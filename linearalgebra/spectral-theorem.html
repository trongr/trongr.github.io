Spectral Theorem

Theorem. Suppoose that $ T $ is a linear operator on a finite-dimensional inner product space $ V $ over $ F $ with the distinct eigenvalues $ \l_1,\ldots,\l_k. $ Assume that $ T $ is normal if $ F = \bC $ and self-adjoint if $ F = \bR. $ For each $ i = 1,\ldots,k, $ let $ W_i $ be the eigenspace of $ T $ corresponding to $ \l_i, $ and let $ T_i $ be the orthogonal projection of $ V $ on $ W_i. $ Then:

  1. $ V = W_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus W_k. $
  2. If $ W_i' = \Oplus_{j\neq i} W_j, $ then $ W_i' = W_i^\perp. $
  3. $ T_i T_j = \d_{ij} T_i $ for $ 1 \leq i,j \leq k. $
  4. $ I = T_1 + \cdots + T_k. $
  5. Spectral decomposition: $ T = \l_1 T_1 + \cdots + \l_k T_k. $

Corollary. If $ F = \bC, $ then $ T $ is normal iff $ T^* = g(T) $ for some polynomial $ g. $
