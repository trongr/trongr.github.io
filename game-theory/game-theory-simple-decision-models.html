Game Theory: Simple Decision Models

Action and Payoff

Definition. A choice of behavior in a single-decision problem is called an action. The set of all possible actions is denoted $ A. $

Definition. A payoff is a function $ \pi: A \longrightarrow \bR $ that assigns a real number to each action $ a \in A. $

Definition. An action $ a^* $ is an optimal action if it maximizes the payoff $ \pi. $ $$ \pi(a^*) \geq \pi(a) $$ for all $ a \in A, $ or equivalently, $$ a^* \in \argmax_{a\in A} \pi(a). $$

Affine transformation on argmax

Definition. An affine transformation changes a function $ \pi $ into $ \pi' $ according to the rule $$ \pi'(a) = \a \pi(a) + \b $$ where $ \a $ and $ \b $ are constants and $ \a > 0. $ An affine transformation is a combination of scalar expansion and a translation.

Theorem. The optimal action is unchanged if the payoffs are altered by an affine transformation. More generally affine transformations do not change the result of argmax.

Proof. Since $ \a > 0, $ if $ a^* $ maximizes $ \pi, $ then \begin{align*} \pi(a^*) & \geq \pi(a) \\ \a \pi(a^*) & \geq \a \pi(a) \\ \a \pi(a^*) + \b & \geq \a \pi(a) + \b \\ \pi'(a^*) & \geq \pi'(a), \\ \end{align*} i.e. $ a^* $ also maximizes $ \pi'. $ In other words, $$ \argmax_{a\in A} \pi'(a) = \argmax_{a\in A} \pi(a). $$
