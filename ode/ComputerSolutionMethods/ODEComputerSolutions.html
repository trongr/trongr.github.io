Differential Equations: Computer Solutions Methods

Euler's Method

We want to solve the first order problem $$ \frac{dx}{dt} = f(x, t) $$ with $ x = x_0 $ at $ t = 0. $ Euler's method involves iteratively solving for $ x_{n+1} $ using $ x_n: $ $$ x_{n+1} = x_n + h f(x_n, t_n), $$ where $ f $ is the slope at $ (x_n, t_n), $ $ h f(x_n, t_n) $ is the change in $ x, $ and $ h $ is the change in $ t, $ i.e. from $ x_0 $ we advance in steps of $ h $ along $ t $ and calculate successively $ x_n. $

Example. Solve $$ \frac{dx}{dt} = ax $$ with $ x = x_0 $ at $ t = 0. $

Solution. We have $$ \begin{align*} x_{n+1} &= x_n + h a x_n \\ &= x_n (1 + h a) \\ &= x_0 (1 + h a)^n. \end{align*} $$ At time $ t = t_n = nh, $ we have $$ \begin{align*} x_{n+1} &= x_0 \bigg(1 + \frac{at}{n}\bigg)^n. \end{align*} $$ Letting $ h \longrightarrow 0 $ while keeping $ t $ fixed, so that $ n \longrightarrow \infty: $ $$ \lim_{h\to 0} x_{n+1} = \lim_{n\to\infty} x_0 \bigg(1 + \frac{at}{n}\bigg)^n = x_0 e^{at}, $$ by a well-known equality.
