Learning and Problem Solving Notes

General Rules

  1. Ask questions. There are no bad questions. Asking stupid questions will lead to asking smart questions.
  2. Try new things. Try old things differently.
  3. Constantly make choices. Decide. Take action. It is better to act, make mistakes, and fix them than to sit and do nothing.
  4. Note specific things. Everything is in the details. Note how the details fit into the big picture.
  5. Take notes. Writing things down makes nebulous ideas seem more real.
  6. Make everything explicit and concrete.
  7. Try and see if you can apply what you've just learned.
  8. Look things up. One thing will lead to another, and it'll all come around.
  9. Pay attention to time and how you're spending it. Measure your progress. Maximize time actually doing things instead of spinning your wheels.
  10. Review and reorganize.
  11. Make it fun and interesting. Add variety.
  12. Don't listen to music when you're reading, writing or thinking deeply. Listen to music when you're performing mechanical / automatic activities that don't require a lot of concentration.
  13. Just keep learning. Things will figure themselves out.
